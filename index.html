Nikopol's Poem {{expandIcon}}

Remember the sight we saw, my soul, that beautiful soft summer morning round a turning in the path, a disgusting carcass on a bed scattered with stones, its legs in the air like a woman in heat, like a fountain with its rhythmical sobs. I can hear it clearly flowing with a long, murmuring sound, but I touch my body in vain to find the wound. I am the vampire of my own heart, one of the great outcasts condemned to eternal laughter who can no longer smile.
Am I dead? I must be dead.
